\begin{abstract}
Agents are moving from short-lived question answering into real-world work:
coding, research, operations, planning, documentation, personal administration,
and long-running projects that cross machines, tools, accounts, and time. The
hard problem is no longer only model capability. It is managing durable facts,
responsibility, trust, context, artifacts, and collaboration between humans and
agents while real work changes underneath them. Kungfu is a product and
infrastructure direction for this problem. It helps people and agents manage
real-world work by providing a local-first runtime fact layer, dual-first human
and agent interfaces, a cross-platform control plane, and an extensible
polyglot architecture. Its central stance is that humans and agents should
cooperate through trusted value, not hidden pressure or brittle workflow
capture. This white paper states the product thesis, explains the KFD
principles behind the trust model, describes the architecture, and outlines the
roadmap from private dogfood to a public product.
\end{abstract}
