\section{Conclusion}

Kungfu is a product thesis about the next layer of agent work. As agents become
more capable, the limiting problem shifts from single-turn intelligence to
durable coordination in the real world. People and agents need facts that do
not drift, trust that starts from those facts, cooperation that begins from
trusted value, and timelines that declare their observer.

This is why Kungfu is not only a technical control plane. It is a statement
about how real-world work should be organized when intelligent participants,
including humans and agents, share responsibility for understanding, acting,
recovering, and leaving evidence. The product should make that world more
legible, not more coercive.

The immediate route is practical: build a local-first product that manages real
work with agents, validate it against real-world use, verify its public
surfaces through KFD and Buildchain, and let extensions grow from a stable core.

If the product succeeds, Kungfu is not merely another agent tool. It becomes a
runtime fact layer and control plane for real-world work with agents, and its
public KFD, Buildchain, trust, runtime, and Hub boundaries become an open Agent
Supply Chain that other products can evaluate and implement without yielding
their product authority. The current proof is intentionally narrower than that
future ecosystem; keeping the distinction visible is part of the trust model.
